Good morning, Chris.

It is a wet one today. Moderate rain coming down, streets slick. You are sitting at about sixty two and a half degrees right now, heading up to a warm-ish high around seventy two point seven. Then dropping all the way down to about forty one and a half tonight. Chance of rain is basically a sure thing, one hundred percent, so if you head out, grab the jacket, grab the umbrella, and maybe the shoes you do not mind getting a little beat up.

Around the world, everybody is buzzing about basketball. Bam Adebayo just dropped an eighty three point game, and guys like LeBron are all over social media calling it historic. In politics, former president Trump and his party are working on a plan to hammer Democrats over crime, bail, and border issues as the election season heats up. And in tech land, Facebook’s parent company just bought a new platform called Moltbook, a kind of social network where artificial intelligence bots talk to each other without people in the mix. So, humans watching robots talk to robots. That is where we are now.

But put all that aside for a second.  

Stop.  

Breathe.

Let your shoulders drop. Unclench your jaw.  

Let today be new, not just a rerun of yesterday’s noise.

“Let us not grow weary in doing good,” Paul wrote, “for at the proper time we will reap a harvest if we do not give up.”  

Jesus said, “Do not worry about tomorrow. Each day has enough trouble of its own.”  

C. S. Lewis once pointed out that God is quietly building us into something we cannot yet imagine, room by room, like a house under renovation, even when all we feel are the hammers and the dust.  

And you know how old school coaches say, “Just win the next play. Not the game. The next play.”  

Hold all of that together for a moment.  

Because today, Chris, I want to talk to you about not leaving before the miracle.

You know that feeling when you are just done.  

Done with the slow habit that is not paying off.  
Done with the job search that keeps going nowhere.  
Done trying to repair a relationship that keeps feeling awkward or one sided.  
Done with your own growth, because you keep seeing the same flaws in the mirror and thinking, “Maybe this is just who I am.”

There is a special kind of exhaustion that sets in when you have been doing the right thing for a while… and nothing seems to be moving.  

You pray.  
You show up.  
You send the resume.  
You apologize again.  
You keep the boundary.  
You go to the gym.  
You stay honest.  

And the heavens are quiet. The inbox is empty. The scale does not move. The other person does not change. You do not feel different.  

That is the place where this line comes in:  

“Do not leave before the miracle.”

People say that in rooms where the stakes are life or death. Rooms where walking out early means walking back into darkness. They say it because they have seen something strange: the moment when you are closest to quitting is often the moment right before something shifts.

Not fireworks.  
Not a movie scene.  
Just a crack in the wall. A small opening.

The problem is, miracles in real life rarely look like miracles while they are happening.  

They look like…  

One more boring, faithful day.  
One more “no” to an old pattern.  
One more “yes” to something that feels small and unimpressive.  
One more phone call that might go nowhere.  
One more quiet prayer you are not even sure is heard.

From the outside, transformation looks like a highlight reel. From the inside, it feels like a long string of Tuesdays where you are tired, tempted, and still choosing.

Think about any big change you have ever made. When you tell the story, you pick out the turning points. The big conversation. The job offer. The day you finally broke down and said, “I cannot do this alone.”  

But if you zoomed in close, what you would really see is a thousand small, hidden choices lined up in a row.  

The miracle was not just the moment.  
The miracle was that you stayed long enough for that moment to arrive.

God tends to work like that. Quietly. Slowly. Under the surface.  

A seed in the ground looks like nothing is happening. It is dark, it is buried, it is alone. From the outside, you might walk by and say, “Dead.” But under the soil, the shell is cracking. Life is pressing against its limits. Roots are reaching down before anything reaches up.

That is you when you feel stuck.  
You are judging the situation from the surface.  
God is working at the root.

The question for today is not, “When will I see the miracle?”  

The question is, “What does it mean for me to stay one more day?”

Because you cannot control the timing. You cannot control the outcome. You cannot control how fast the other person grows, how quickly the market responds, how soon your heart feels light again.  

You can control your side of the street.  

You can control your honesty.  
You can control your effort.  
You can control your willingness to be guided.

So maybe today, staying looks like this:

You are tempted to ghost that opportunity because you are embarrassed or tired. Instead, you send one more email. One more application. One more follow up. Not ten. Just one.

You are tempted to withdraw from that person you are trying to reconcile with, because the last talk was awkward and you feel exposed. Instead, you send a simple, kind text. “Thinking of you. Hope you are well.” You keep the door cracked.

You are tempted to break a promise you made to yourself. The food choice. The drink. The website. The purchase. The anger. Whatever it is for you. Instead, you say, “Not today.” Not “never again.” Just, “Not today.”

You are tempted to stop talking to God because you feel silly repeating the same prayer. Instead, you give Him thirty honest seconds. “I am tired. I want to quit. I need help.” That is enough.

You do not have to feel strong to stay.  
You just have to be willing not to run.

There is a strange power in narrowing your focus down to this single day.  

Not, “Can I do this for the next year?”  
But, “Can I do the next right thing for the next twenty four hours?”  

Can you be honest, just for today.  
Show up, just for today.  
Be open, just for today.  

Tomorrow will bring its own challenges and its own grace. You do not have to solve that yet.

There is a line in the old stories where God provides daily bread. Not weekly bread. Not lifetime bread. Just enough for that day. If the people tried to hoard it, it spoiled. The lesson was simple. Trust Me today. I will meet you again tomorrow.

That is the invitation for you this morning.  

Do not demand to see the whole plan.  
Ask for enough light for the next step.

The truth is, the urge to quit usually does not come when nothing is happening. It comes when something is happening that you cannot yet see. Pressure increases right before a breakthrough. Resistance spikes right before growth.  

You start to feel more tempted, not less.  
You feel more restless, not calmer.  
Old patterns start knocking louder.  

That is not proof that you are failing. That is often proof that you are getting close to the edge of an old life. And the part of you that is comfortable with how things were… wants you to step back.

So when you feel that today, do not panic. Name it.  

“Okay. This is the moment where I usually bail.”  

And then, very quietly, choose not to.

One more honest conversation.  
One more small act of discipline.  
One more prayer that feels weak but is actually brave.  

You do not have to know how the miracle will look. You just have to decide you are not walking out before the curtain lifts.

Chris, if there is one area of your life where you are right on the edge today… the edge of giving up, giving in, or going numb… I want you to pick a tiny act of staying.

Make one phone call you have been avoiding.  
Write one paragraph of the thing you keep postponing.  
Take one walk instead of one escape.  
Say one sentence to God instead of going silent.  

Let that be your way of saying, “I am still here. I am not leaving before my miracle.”

You do your part today. Let God handle the timing. Let tomorrow worry about tomorrow.  

Your job is simple.  

Stay.  
Stand.  
Show up.  

One more day.

Now, go get after it.  

“Do not leave before the miracle.” — Unknown
