Good morning, Chris.

Clear skies over you this Thursday. Right now it is sitting at about thirty four degrees, a cold, crisp start. We are heading up to around sixty seven this afternoon, with a low just above thirty three tonight. Almost no chance of rain, about one percent, so it is one of those bright, clean days where the air wakes you up for free.

In the world, the Senate is locked in that marathon election debate, and it is actually splitting Republicans more than Democrats right now. Traders are watching stock futures climb a bit as everyone waits to see what the Federal Reserve does with interest rates. And overseas, tension is red hot, with reports that Iran’s intelligence minister has been killed and missiles are flying between Iran, Israel, and the Gulf.

But put all that aside for a second.  
Stop.  
Breathe.

Let your shoulders drop.  
Unclench your jaw.  
You are here. It is morning. You have been given another shot at this.

“Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for men.” That is Paul to the Colossians.

“Relying on God has to begin all over again every day, as if nothing had yet been done.” That is C. S. Lewis.

And I think of coaches telling their players, “Do your job. Play the next play. The score will take care of itself.”

So this morning, hold this in your mind:  
You bring your best.  
God owns the outcome.

All you can do is the best you can do.

Let us sit with that.

Because that line can either set you free, or it can put you to sleep.

On one side, people use it as a shrug. “Well, I did my best,” when they know they coasted, cut corners, stayed comfortable. That is not what we are talking about. That is just a soft pillow for a guilty conscience.

On the other side, that same sentence can be a release valve for a mind that never stops grinding. A mind that replays every conversation, every decision, every number. A mind that thinks, “If I had just pushed a little harder, been a little smarter, controlled one more variable, then the result would have been perfect.”

You know that place.  
The one where you are doing well by any sane standard, but inside you still feel like you are behind, or late, or failing.

So let us draw a line between coasting and peace.

Doing your honest best today means this. You tell the truth to yourself about the effort. Not the story. The effort. You ask, “Given my energy, my knowledge, my time, my responsibilities today… did I actually lean in? Or did I hide?”

If you showed up, prepared, focused, and you moved the ball as far as you honestly could… then “all you can do is the best you can do” is not an excuse. It is a boundary. It is where your responsibility ends, and God’s begins.

The torment comes when you try to own both sides.  
Your effort and the outcome.  
Your obedience and the harvest.

There is a reason the old wisdom says, “Give us this day our daily bread.” Not “this quarter’s bread.” Not “this retirement’s bread.” Daily. One day at a time. One decision at a time.

That is not small thinking. That is how you build frightening consistency.

When you shrink your focus down to what is right in front of you, something powerful happens. The noise drops. The drama drops. The stories about “what if this fails” and “what if I never get there” start to lose their grip.

You are just asking, “What is the next right action?”  
Not the perfect action.  
The right one.

You do that with your body.  
You do that with your work.  
You do that with your relationships.

And when your mind tries to sprint thirty steps ahead, you pull it back. “No. Today. This hour. This call. This set. This email. This conversation.”

That is where your strength lives. Not in the fantasies about the future, not in the autopsy of the past. In the present moment, where you can actually choose.

Now, underneath all of this is trust.

If you do not believe anyone else is in charge, then of course you will try to control everything. Of course you will obsess over outcomes. Because if you do not, who will?

But if you really believe that God is not just out there, but here, involved, wise, and good… then the job description shifts.

Your job is faithfulness.  
His job is results.

You plant. You water. He gives the growth.  
You prepare well, you execute with integrity, you show up when you say you will.  
You apologize when you are wrong, you listen when you would rather talk, you choose the harder right over the easier wrong.

That is your side of the street.

You are not the architect of every domino that falls after that. You are not the judge of how fast the harvest must come. You are not required to carry the weight of every scenario that might happen two months from now.

When you try to pick up that weight, that is when the anxiety spikes. That is when your emotions start hijacking the day. Because you are trying to control things that are, by design, not yours to control.

So here is the move for today. Make this practical.

Pick one area where your mind keeps looping, where you are obsessing about how it will turn out. Maybe it is work. A project, a deal, a promotion. Maybe it is money. A number you cannot stop staring at. Maybe it is a relationship. A conversation you are afraid to have, or an outcome you are trying to engineer.

Name it. Be specific.

Then, on paper, write down the two or three actions that are truly your responsibility today. Not everything you could possibly do. Just what is actually in your hands between now and bedtime.

For work, it might be: prepare thoroughly for one meeting, send that one clear email you have been avoiding, block ninety minutes of deep, undistracted work.

For money, it might be: update the budget honestly, make one concrete decision to cut or adjust, move a small amount toward savings or debt.

For a relationship, it might be: reach out with one honest message, listen without defending in one conversation, pray for that person by name.

That is it. Two or three. Your side of the street.

Then, and this is the hard part, you consciously release the rest.

You say, “Lord, I am bringing you my best today. I am not holding back, and I am not coasting. I am doing my work with all my heart. And I am trusting you with what I cannot see, and what I cannot force.”

You might have to say that more than once.  
You might have to say it every hour.

But that is how you live free. High standards, low obsession. Full effort, open hands.

You are not lowering the bar. You are refusing to let the bar become your god.

So as you step into this clear, cold morning, remember this: you are not called to be perfect. You are called to be faithful. You are not asked to control the story. You are asked to tell the truth with your life, one decision at a time.

Bring your best.  
Then let the day be what it will be.

Now, go get after it.

Quote of the day: “All you can do is the best you can do.” — Anonymous.
