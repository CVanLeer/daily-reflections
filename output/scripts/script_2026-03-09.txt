Good morning, Chris.

It is a clear start out there. Sitting right around sixty two degrees this morning. We will climb to about seventy six this afternoon, with skies staying mainly clear. Low tonight just under sixty. There is a small chance of rain, about thirty percent, but for the most part it should be a bright, dry Monday.

Around the world, things are not quite so calm. Bahrain is accusing Iran of hitting a desalination plant with a drone, which is a worrying sign that the regional conflict is touching more civilian infrastructure. In Norway, police say an explosion hit near the United States embassy in Oslo, thankfully with no injuries reported. And in sports, fight fans are buzzing after Charles Oliveira shocked Max Holloway at Ultimate Fighting Championship three twenty six, using relentless grappling to take that “B M F” title.

But put all that aside for a second.  

Stop.  

Breathe.

Let your shoulders drop.  

Let your jaw unclench.  

You are here. It is Monday. And God has already seen the whole week you are about to walk into.

“Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds. Because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance.” That is James, chapter one.

Jesus said, “In this world you will have trouble. But take heart. I have overcome the world.”

C. S. Lewis wrote, “Hardships often prepare ordinary people for an extraordinary destiny.”

And you know how the great coaches talk. “Everybody wants to win on Saturday. The question is, who is willing to suffer on Monday.”

So, Chris, just for a moment, ask God, “Show me what you want to strengthen in me this week. Not just what I want you to fix.”

Let that settle in.  

Now let us talk about this week you are about to build.

You and I both know there is a fantasy version of Monday we secretly hope for.  

All the traffic lights are green. Every email is short and kind. The calendar magically opens up. No conflict. No surprises. Every plan goes exactly the way you mapped it in your head last night.

It is smooth. It is painless. It is… honestly, kind of boring.

And then there is the week that would actually forge something in you.  

The week that would grow courage. Patience. Clarity.  

That week does not look like a highlight reel. It looks like:

The meeting that goes sideways.  
The person who misunderstands you.  
The project that hits a wall.  
The moment you are tired and still choose to keep your word.  

You cannot wish for a strong character and an easy life. Those two do not belong in the same sentence.

So here is the invitation for this Monday. Do not just let the week happen to you. Pre decide who you are going to be when, not if, things get rough.

Instead of, “Why is this happening to me,” shift it to, “What part of me is this trying to strengthen.”

You get cut off in traffic. You can say, “Why is everyone an idiot,” or you can say, “Looks like my patience is on the training schedule today.”

A co worker drops the ball and it lands on your plate. You can say, “Why does this always end up my problem,” or, “Okay, this is my chance to practice leadership instead of complaining. How do I bring order without shaming them.”

A plan you were counting on changes last minute. You can say, “Of course, nothing ever works out,” or, “This is my flexibility drill. Can I adapt without losing my peace.”

That one question, “What part of me is this trying to strengthen,” turns random annoyances into a personal training plan.

But you are not just reacting. You are also designing.  

You are the architect of this week more than you think.

Look at your calendar, even mentally, right now. Ask yourself three things.

First. Where am I choosing difficulty on purpose.

If you want to be stronger, you do not wait for heavy things to fall on you. You go to the weight rack and pick them up. The same is true for your character.

Maybe for you that is scheduling the hard conversation you have been avoiding. Putting it on the calendar, not “sometime,” but Wednesday at three o’clock.

Maybe it is blocking off a focused ninety minutes for the deep work that actually moves the needle, instead of hiding in busy work.

Maybe it is getting up a bit earlier this week to move your body, even when the bed feels like the promised land.

Chosen difficulty is where you grow the most. Because there, you are not a victim. You are a trainee.

Second. Where are my boundaries too soft.

If you do not set the shape of your week, other people’s urgency will. Then it feels like life is just happening to you, and you are constantly behind.

So you build small, firm lines.  

A start time and end time for work.  
Times where you are present with family and your phone is not the main character.  
A limit to how many late night commitments you will say yes to.

This is not about being rigid. It is about protecting the energy you need to carry the weight you have chosen.

Third. Where am I pretending I can do everything.

You are strategic by nature. You know not all tasks are equal. Some are worth your full attention. Some should be delegated. Some should be deleted.

So ask, “What are the two or three things this week that, if done well, would make the biggest difference.” Circle those in your mind. Let those be your non negotiables. Let other things flex around them.

That is how you move from being crushed by random chaos to shouldering meaningful difficulty.

Now, let us talk about leadership. At work. At home. In your circle.

Leadership is not a title. It is going first into discomfort.

It is being the one who says, “I was wrong. That one is on me.” That is uncomfortable. But that is how trust is built.

It is being the one who has the honest conversation. “Hey, can we talk about how we are working together. Something is off, and I want it to be better.” That is uncomfortable. But that is how relationships deepen.

It is being the one who admits, “I do not know, but I will find out.” That is uncomfortable. But that is how clarity grows.

Every time you go first into that discomfort, you are training the exact character you say you want. Courage. Humility. Steadiness.

And here is the quiet part. When you embrace a demanding week on purpose, it is not just about you.

Your family watches how you handle stress. Your team watches how you respond when the plan breaks. The people around you take their emotional temperature from you.

If you treat every problem like a personal insult, they will too. If you treat every problem like a training ground, slowly, they start doing the same.

So picture this week like a gym God built for your soul.  

He knows exactly which muscles are weak.  

He is not punishing you. He is preparing you.

The conflict that pops up.  
The delay that frustrates you.  
The opportunity that scares you a little.  

They are not proof that your planning failed. They are the raw material for a stronger you.

So as you walk into this Monday, here is a simple pattern you can carry.

When something hard hits, pause for one breath. Just one.  

Inhale. “Lord, I receive this.”  

Exhale. “Show me what you want to grow in me.”

Then act. Not from panic. From purpose.

Build the week that tests you, on purpose. Choose some hard things, set some real boundaries, and be the one who goes first into discomfort. That is how you end up, Friday night, tired in the best way. Not drained and resentful, but used well.

Now, go get after it.
