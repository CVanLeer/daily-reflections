If you want to see your real priorities, do three quick scans.

First, your mornings. What happens in the first ninety minutes after you wake up? If God is actually first, does he get time, attention, and stillness before the world starts screaming? Or does your phone get first access to your nervous system?

Second, your energy peaks. When your brain is sharp, usually late morning for you, what do you give that to? Do the hardest, most leverage heavy problems get that slot? Or do you burn it on inbox, metrics grazing, and dashboards?

Third, your fatigue. When you are tired, what do you default to? Scrolling, random browsing, one more “quick check”? That is your comfort priority. The thing you run to when the mask is off.

Here is the thing, Chris. There is no moral victory in having “aspirational priorities” on paper while your habits serve a totally different god. The goal is alignment. Saying, “My habits are out of sync with what I say I value” is not self condemnation. It is diagnostics.

So, how do you use this today, not in some vague “someday I will be better” way?

Let us turn it into moves.

Move one. Name your real top three for this season. Not forever. Just this current stretch.

Something like: walk with God in honesty, protect your health, and build durable systems at work. That might look like Forge and Nexus and this whole multi agent orchestration future you are leaning into.

Say them out loud. Write them down. If you cannot name them, your habits will be random by default.

Move two. Build one proof habit for each priority. One small, non negotiable behavior that makes the priority visible.

For faith, that might be: ten minutes of scripture and silence before you touch your phone. Not heroic. Just consistent. You are telling your nervous system, “God speaks before the world does.”

For health, that might be: a daily walk, or a short training block, even on busy days. Not “when I have time.” It is the thing you schedule around, like a meeting with someone you respect.

For systems and leverage at work, that might be: one ninety minute deep work block, device quiet, on the highest leverage system of the day. Maybe today that is Forge, or tightening up Nexus command center so it runs clean from your iPhone, or pushing the last pieces of that catering digest rollout so it stops being a half done loop in your head.

The rule is simple. If a priority does not have a habit attached to it, it is just marketing.

Move three. Audit and cut the fake priorities. These are the habits that are stealing from what you say you value.

Maybe that is checking every Tattle note the second it lands, instead of batching them once a day and turning them into a simple improvement loop. Maybe it is grazing on dashboards or logs when you are bored, instead of documenting one process or automating one step in the pipeline.

You do not need to fix everything today. Just pick one thief habit and put a speed bump in front of it. For example, no email before your morning God time. Or no Slack during your deep work block. Or phone stays in another room for the first hour of the day.

The point is not perfection. The point is direction. Every small shift is a re alignment between what you say and how you live.

And here is the deeper layer. From a faith lens, your habits are not just about productivity. They are liturgy. Repeated acts of worship. You are always worshiping something. Comfort, control, status, or God. Your habits show you which altar you are actually at.

From the stoic side, habits are how you pre decide your behavior so you are not at the mercy of moods and headlines. You do not wait to feel brave. You build the habit of doing the next right thing, even when you do not feel like it.

From the strategic, INTJ side, habits are your personal infrastructure. They let you scale yourself. You cannot be everywhere, but your systems and routines can quietly run while you focus on the next thing only you can do.

So today, do not just think, “I should have better habits.” That is vague and useless. Ask a sharper question. “What do my current habits say I actually worship? And what is one habit I can change today to move that worship back where it belongs?”
